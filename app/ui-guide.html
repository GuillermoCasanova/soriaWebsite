Helper Classes

We have a number of helper classes that allow us to quickly style elements throughout the application without adding unnecessary bloat and complexity to our CSS. This also allows us to keep our CSS specificity low.

Spacing

Padding and margin can be applied by using the classes mt, mr, mb, ml for margin and pt, pr, pb, pl for padding (from 0-5). For example, adding .mt1 to a div gives it a margin top of 6px, mb2 would be a margin bottom of 24px, and so forth. The classes .mtrbl (top right bottom left) and .ptrbl apply the specified value to all sides of the element, and .mtrbl0 and .ptrbl0 removing all margin and padding from the element respectively.

Top and bottom margins
  • .mt0 for removing margin at top
  • .mt1 for margin top of 6px
  • .mt2 for margin top of 12px
  • .mt3 for margin top of 24px
  • .mt4 for margin top of 36px
  • .mt5 for margin top of 48px
  • .mb0 for removing margin at bottom
  • .mb1 for margin bottom of 6px
  • .mb2 for margin bottom of 12px
  • .mb3 for margin bottom of 24px
  • .mb4 for margin bottom of 36px
  • .mb5 for margin bottom of 48px
Top and bottom paddings
  • .pt0 for removing padding at top
  • .pt1 for padding top of 6px
  • .pt2 for padding top of 12px
  • .pt3 for padding top of 24px
  • .pt4 for padding top of 36px
  • .pt5 for padding top of 48px
  • .pb0 for removing padding at bottom

These classes help with adding dropshadows to elements to differentiate their distance from the page. They are also available as mixins if you do not wish to place them in your HTML.

Class Description
